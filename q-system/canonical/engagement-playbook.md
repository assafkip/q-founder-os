# Social Engagement Playbook

> Rules for commenting, DMs, and relationship building on social platforms.

## Core Rules

1. **Never pitch your product in comments** unless directly asked
2. **Max 3-4 sentences** per comment
3. **1 comment per person per week** (unless they reply)
4. **Every engagement logged** to tracker (Notion or local file)
5. **Comment style adapted to relationship type:**
   - Investors: domain insight, market perspective
   - Practitioners: peer validation, shared experience
   - Connectors: amplification, bridge-building

## Comment Styles

### Insight Style
Add a non-obvious observation. Connect their post to something they haven't considered. Show you think about this deeply.

### Connector Style
Bridge their idea to someone else's work or a related trend. "This connects to what [person] was saying about..."

### Question Style
Ask a genuine question that advances the conversation. Not "great post!" but a question that shows you engaged with the content.

## Connection Request Styles

Three styles. Pick based on context. Max 300 characters.

### Post Reference
Best when: they have a recent post that aligns with your domain.
```
I saw your post about [specific thing]. That matches exactly
what I'm seeing in [your context]. Would love to connect.
```

### Signal Share
Best when: a signal, article, or data point is directly relevant to their work.
```
I came across [specific signal/article] that's relevant to
your work on [their domain]. Thought you'd want to see it.
```

### Advice-Seeking
Best when: senior leader, no recent post to reference, cold Tier A lead. Most effective style for senior people who don't post often.

Why it works: you're not offering anything they can reject. You're saying "you know more than me." People accept connection requests to give advice, not to be sold to. They self-discover the problem when they give you their perspective.

```
I'm working on [problem space] and trying to get the mental
model right. You've lived this problem. Would value your
perspective on where it breaks down.
```

Rules for advice-seeking:
- No product name. No pitch. No value offer. The ask IS the hook.
- Start with "I" (not their name)
- Name the problem space, not the solution
- Acknowledge their expertise specifically ("you've lived this", "you've seen this at scale")

When NOT to use:
- Warm connection or mutual contact exists (use intro path)
- They recently posted something you can reference (post reference is stronger)
- Already connected (advice-seeking is for first touch only)

## DM Rules

- Start with "I" not their name (never "Hey Sarah, been thinking...")
- One ask OR one question. Never both.
- No "hope you're well" or "hope this finds you"
- Get to the point in 2-3 sentences
- Sign off with just your first name (no Best/Cheers/Regards)

## Engagement Tracking

Log every engagement with:
- Person name
- Platform
- Type (comment / DM / connection request)
- Content summary
- Date
- Follow-up date (7 days out)
