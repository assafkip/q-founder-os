The Origin Story

A review approved output that would have locked out firefighters.

The generated policy was technically perfect. Every expert reviewer signed off. The syntax was valid, the logic was sound, the coverage looked comprehensive.

One problem: it assumed infrastructure the customer didn't have. It referenced an identity provider they never deployed. It would have locked frontline responders out of their own systems during an emergency.

Every reviewer evaluated the output in a vacuum. Nobody checked whether it fit the customer who was going to use it.

This skill exists so that never happens again. No review runs without a customer profile. No output ships without the customer persona signing off.

Automatic Deviation Rules

Four ways output goes wrong

These fire automatically during every review. When triggered, the persona stops and flags the issue before continuing.

Infrastructure Assumption

DEVIATION_01

Output assumes a service, license, or integration the customer doesn't have. The review adds a triage gate or skips the output entirely.

FLAG References Azure AD conditional access -- customer uses local Active Directory only

Dangerous Default

DEVIATION_02

Output would break the customer's environment. Lock out users, delete data, block legitimate access. The review adjusts to safe defaults.

FLAG MFA enforcement would lock out field crews without smartphone access

Coverage Lie

DEVIATION_03

Output claims "all devices" or "fully covered" when coverage is partial. Unmanaged devices exist. Integrations aren't configured. The review qualifies the claim.

FLAG "Full endpoint coverage" -- 340 of 812 devices are unmanaged

Expertise Assumption

DEVIATION_04

Output requires knowledge the customer's contact doesn't have, with no explanation or guidance path. The review adds plain-language instructions.

FLAG Requires KQL proficiency -- customer contact is a network admin, not a SOC analyst

Deployment Reality

The customer profile is mandatory

No review runs without it. The profile captures what the customer actually has deployed, not what the documentation assumes. Managed vs. unmanaged devices. Infrastructure present vs. absent. Integrations configured vs. not configured.

Non-Negotiable

The Customer Persona

Domain experts review FOR the customer. The customer persona reviews AS the customer. It is constructed from the profile's contact_background and contact_constraints fields and asks four questions:

"Can I do this with my resources?"

"Do I know what to do if something goes wrong?"

"Does this make me feel stupid?"

"Would I pay for this?"

Veto power. The customer persona's "would use" verdict is the ultimate gate. If domain experts all pass but the customer persona says no, the output does not ship. Technically correct is not enough.
