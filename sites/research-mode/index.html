The problem

LLMs hallucinate. That's not a bug report. That's the baseline.

When your AI assistant is writing pitch decks, researching competitors, and drafting investor briefs, one wrong fact can kill your credibility. A fabricated statistic in a board deck. A competitor capability that doesn't exist. A market size number pulled from nowhere.

You can't fact-check every sentence. You need the model to police itself.

Research Mode activates three constraints -- pulled directly from Anthropic's documentation on reducing hallucinations -- and enforces them as a system prompt. It's not a wrapper. It's a toggle.

Three constraints activate simultaneously

Each one is simple. Together, they change how Claude handles facts.

Say "I don't know"

No guessing. No inferring. If there's no credible source, Claude says so instead of generating a confident-sounding answer from nothing.

Cite everything

Every claim must reference a file, URL, paper, or named source. Unsourced claims get retracted. No exceptions.

Quote first, then analyze

Responses are grounded in word-for-word quotes from source material, not paraphrased summaries. Analysis follows evidence, not the other way around.

Same question. Different output.

Ask about the Change Healthcare breach. See what changes.

Without Research Mode

"The Change Healthcare breach in 2024 was caused by a sophisticated ransomware attack that exploited a critical vulnerability in their payment processing infrastructure, affecting over 100 million patients."

Confident tone Partially wrong No sources Fabricated specifics
With Research Mode

"According to the HHS OCR investigation report [source], the Change Healthcare breach involved the ALPHV/BlackCat ransomware group gaining access through compromised credentials on a Citrix remote access portal that lacked MFA. 'The threat actor leveraged compromised credentials to remotely access a Change Healthcare Citrix portal' [HHS breach report, Oct 2024]. I don't have verified data on the exact patient count -- estimates range from 100M+ but the final figure hasn't been confirmed by UHG."

Named sources Direct quotes Honest about unknowns Verifiable claims

What it doesn't do

Common assumptions, addressed.

Not always-on

It's a toggle. Turn it on for research, investor prep, and competitive analysis. Turn it off for creative work, brainstorming, and code generation.

Not slow

Claude still uses tools in parallel and works efficiently. Citation discipline doesn't mean waiting around. It means the output you get is grounded.

Not restrictive

You can synthesize across sources, draw connections, and generate new insights. But the inputs must be grounded. Analysis on top of evidence, not on top of air.

Install in 2 commands

Add from the Claude Code plugin marketplace. Takes about 10 seconds.

Step 1 Add from marketplace
/plugin marketplace add assafkip/research-mode
Step 2 Install the plugin
/plugin install research-mode@assafkip-research-mode

Usage

Activate research mode
/research-mode:research
Activate with a topic
/research-mode:research what caused the Change Healthcare breach

Say "exit research mode" to turn it off and return to normal Claude behavior.
