How It Works

Eight systems working together to produce writing only you could have written.

Voice DNA Extraction

Analyzes your real writing samples to build a unique voice profile. Captures vocabulary, sentence rhythm, opening patterns, closing patterns, and tone shifts. Not a style guide. A fingerprint.

Anti-AI Detection

Every output is tested against perplexity, burstiness, and the "who wrote this?" test. If a reader would guess AI, the skill rewrites it. Passes detection tools by not sounding like a model.

The Scar Pattern

Every piece of writing anchors in real operational experience. Not "organizations struggle with X." Instead: "At Google, I watched this happen firsthand." Scars, not abstractions.

The Contrast Pattern

Sharp either/or statements, not gradients. "X isn't Y. It's Z." Forces clarity. Eliminates the mushy middle ground where AI loves to hide.

The Question-as-Dagger

Questions that expose uncomfortable truths, not drive engagement. Not "What do you think?" but questions that make the reader stop and reconsider their assumptions.

50+ Banned AI Words

A hard-enforced blocklist. "Delve," "comprehensive," "leverage," "utilize," and 46 more. If a word screams AI, it never reaches your output. Zero tolerance.

Structural Anti-Patterns

Detects and blocks uniform sentence length, formulaic closings, "furthermore/moreover" openers, colon overuse, and everything-in-threes. The patterns AI uses without knowing it.

Per-Channel Voice Rules

Different registers for LinkedIn posts, emails, DMs, and long-form articles. Your post voice is not your email voice. The skill knows the difference and adapts automatically.

The Banned List

50+ words that instantly mark your writing as AI-generated. The skill enforces zero tolerance. None of these will ever appear in your output.

Plus: "I think," "I believe," "perhaps," "arguably," "it seems like," "I'm excited to announce," "thrilled to share," "In today's rapidly evolving..."

The Writing Rules

Six hard constraints that shape every sentence. Not guidelines. Rules.

01

Short Sentences. Declarative.

Average 8-15 words. Some shorter. Rarely over 20. One idea per sentence. Paragraphs: 1-3 sentences max. White space is a feature, not a bug.

02

No Hedging. Ever.

"I think," "I believe," "perhaps," "arguably" are all banned. State positions directly. If uncertain, say "I don't know yet." That's more honest than hedging.

03

The Scar Pattern

Anchor in real operational experience. "At [Company], I watched four teams fight the same problem." Not "Organizations often struggle with cross-team coordination challenges."

04

The Contrast Pattern

Sharp either/or statements. "X isn't Y. It's Z." Eliminate gradients. The mushy middle is where forgettable writing lives.

05

The Question-as-Dagger

Questions that expose uncomfortable truths. Not "What do you think?" or "Agree?" Questions that make the reader stop scrolling and reconsider what they assumed was true.

06

Channel-Aware Endings

Social posts end with a direct question or sharp statement. Articles end with a reflective question that reframes the whole piece. Emails end with one clear ask. Never "Thoughts?"

The Detection Test

Every piece of output is measured against three criteria before it reaches you.

Test 1

Perplexity

Is the skill always picking the most predictable next word? AI writing is low-perplexity. Humans are surprising. The skill checks that word choices are not the statistically obvious ones.

Test 2

Burstiness

Are sentences varying enough in length? AI writes at uniform length. Humans mix four-word punches with longer developing sentences. The skill enforces variation.

Test 3

"Who Wrote This?"

Remove the byline. Could a reader tell which specific person wrote this? Or would they guess "some AI"? If the answer is the latter, the skill rewrites until it passes.

Quality Gate

Seven checks. All must pass. No exceptions.

CHECK 01
Scar Test

At least one paragraph anchors in real experience

CHECK 02
Contrast Test

At least one sharp contrast pattern present

CHECK 03
Specificity Test

CHECK 04
Filler Test

Zero banned AI words in the output

CHECK 05
Sentence Test

Length varies between short and long

CHECK 06
Paragraph Test

At least one single-sentence paragraph

CHECK 07
Personality Test

Remove the byline. Can you tell WHO wrote this?

RESULT
All Pass = Ship

Any fail = rewrite that section. No partial passes.

Get Started

Three steps. Five minutes. Your voice, enforced on every output.

Step 1 — Add the skill to your project

Copy the .claude/skills/founder-voice/ directory into your Claude Code project. The skill includes:

.claude/skills/founder-voice/
  SKILL.md              # Core rules engine
  references/
    voice-dna.md        # Your voice profile (fill this in)
    writing-samples.md  # Your real writing (paste 5-10 samples)
Step 2 — Build your Voice DNA

Open voice-dna.md and fill in your voice profile: background, core voice, natural vocabulary, words you never use, opening/closing patterns, and per-channel register.

Paste 5-10 real writing samples into writing-samples.md. LinkedIn posts, emails, articles. The more real samples, the more accurate the voice match.

Step 3 — Activate in CLAUDE.md

Add this line to your project's CLAUDE.md: 

Every piece of written output must use the founder voice skill.
Read `.claude/skills/founder-voice/SKILL.md` before generating
any written content. No exceptions.

That's it. Every output Claude Code generates will now go through your voice rules, banned word list, structural anti-patterns, and quality gate.
