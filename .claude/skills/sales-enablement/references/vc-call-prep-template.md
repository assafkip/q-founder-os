# VC Call Prep Brief Template

> Every VC call brief must make the founder feel like THIS specific VC is the one they want to work with.
> Not generic investor excitement. Specific to their portfolio, thesis, team, and what makes them different.
> Used by: `/q-morning` Step 2 (meeting prep), ad-hoc call prep requests

---

## Required Research (BEFORE writing the brief)

For every VC call, research and include:

1. **The person** - full name, title, background, previous roles, what they're passionate about, public talks/podcasts/articles
2. **The fund** - size, stage, thesis, sectors, geographic focus, fund vintage
3. **Portfolio companies** - especially anything adjacent to {{YOUR_COMPANY}} (your target market vertical). List specific names.
4. **What they pride themselves on** - what do they say publicly about how they help founders? What makes them different? Check website, LinkedIn, blog posts, press.
5. **Recent investments/news** - last 6 months. What they're actively excited about.
6. **Team/partners** - who else is on the team, venture partners, advisors. Any backgrounds relevant to {{YOUR_COMPANY}}'s market.
7. **Check size and style** - do they lead? Follow? What size checks at what stages?
8. **Connection to security/enterprise** - even tangential (portfolio companies, partner backgrounds, LP base)

---

## Brief Structure

### Section 1: WHO IS [NAME]
- Background, career path, investing style
- What motivates them personally (not just professionally)
- How they work with founders (hands-on vs passive)

### Section 2: THE FUND
- Numbers: fund size, check size, stage, portfolio count
- Team and venture partners with relevant backgrounds highlighted
- LP base if known (opens doors?)

### Section 3: WHY [FUND] SPECIFICALLY MATTERS FOR {{YOUR_COMPANY}}
This is the section that makes the call genuine. 3-5 specific hooks:

Each hook must follow this format:
- **The fact:** what you found in their portfolio/thesis/team
- **The connection:** how it specifically maps to {{YOUR_COMPANY}}
- **What to say:** a ready-to-use line the founder can deliver naturally

Hooks should come from:
- Portfolio companies in adjacent spaces (name them)
- Venture partners whose backgrounds map to {{YOUR_COMPANY}}'s buyer
- Their stated thesis language that aligns with {{YOUR_COMPANY}}'s positioning
- Recent investments that show where their head is RIGHT NOW
- Fund structure/size that makes {{YOUR_COMPANY}} a good fit

### Section 4: CALL FLOW (timed, top-to-bottom readable)

**This is the most important section.** The founder reads this top to bottom before the call. Every hook from Section 3 must be BAKED INTO the conversation text below, not referenced separately. The founder should be able to read this once and walk into the call ready.

Format: timed blocks with headers (OPEN, PROBLEM, SOLUTION, WHY THIS FUND, THE ASK, THEIR QUESTIONS). Each block contains the actual words the founder would say, with hooks woven naturally into the flow.

Rules:
- All portfolio references, venture partner bridges, and thesis connections appear AS SPOKEN TEXT in the flow
- No "remember to mention X" or "reference Y here." The text IS the reference.
- Include at least one genuine question about their portfolio or thesis (shows "I picked you on purpose")
- Leave 5+ minutes for their questions at the end

### Section 5: IF THEY PUSH BACK
Objection | Response format. Tailored to THIS VC's likely concerns (fund focus, stage, sector fit). Written as spoken text, not bullet-point advice.

**NO DO/DO NOT SECTION.** Everything the founder needs is in the call flow text above. If a behavior matters, it's embedded in the conversation, not listed as advice the founder won't reference mid-call.

---

## Rules (ENFORCED)

1. **Every hook must be specific.** "Your portfolio aligns with our space" is garbage. "[Specific example connecting their portfolio company to your value prop]" is a hook.

2. **Research their most recent investment.** That's where their head is right now. Reference it.

3. **Find the venture partner bridge.** Most funds have partners/advisors whose backgrounds map to {{YOUR_COMPANY}}'s enterprise buyer. Name them. Explain why that matters.

4. **Use their language back to them.** If they say "governance from day one" on their website, use "governance" in your pitch. If they talk about "infrastructure layers," mirror that framing.

5. **Ask about their portfolio.** Showing genuine curiosity about their investments is the highest-signal thing a founder can do. It says: I picked you on purpose.

6. **The brief is a workbench, not a script.** The founder reads it, absorbs the hooks, and has a natural conversation. It's not meant to be read aloud.

7. **No generic VC flattery.** "You have an amazing portfolio" means nothing. "Overmind was your most recent investment, and it's the agent-level version of what we're building at the organizational level" means everything.
