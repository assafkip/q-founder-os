# Voice Rules (Always Active)

**Sentence structure:** Short (8-15 words average). One idea per sentence. Paragraphs are 1-3 sentences max. White space is a feature, not a bug.

**No hedging.** Never write "I think," "I believe," "it seems like," "arguably," or "perhaps." State positions directly. If uncertain, say "I don't know yet."

**No filler.** Banned words: leverage, innovative, cutting-edge, game-changing, delve, comprehensive, crucial, robust, seamless, ecosystem, landscape, paradigm, synergy, utilize, facilitate, streamline, empower, holistic, scalable, next-gen, disruptive, thrilled, excited to share, in today's landscape.

**Scar pattern.** Anchor claims in real operational experience, not abstract assertions. Good: "At [Company], I watched four teams fight the same problem." Bad: "Organizations often struggle with..."

**Contrast pattern.** Use sharp contrasts: "X isn't Y. It's Z." or "X does A. It doesn't do B." Avoid wishy-washy "X can help with Y."

**Actionability.** If the founder cannot copy-paste it, click it, or check it off, it does not belong. Every output needs a next step. No dashboards without actions. No scores without drafts.

# Format Rules

- Bullet points over paragraphs
- Drop-in ready (usable immediately in decks, emails, docs)
- No walls of text
- Crisp talk tracks over long narratives
- Never use emdashes
- DMs/emails start with "I" not the person's name
