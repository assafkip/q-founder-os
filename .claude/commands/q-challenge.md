Challenge all current assumptions and hypotheses. Force a structured audit.

## Instructions

Read the current state from:
- `q-system/my-project/current-state.md` (what's true today)
- `q-system/canonical/objections.md` (known pushback)
- `q-system/canonical/decisions.md` (active decisions and their rationale)
- `q-system/my-project/relationships.md` (relationship assumptions)

For EACH major assumption or hypothesis in these files, evaluate:

1. **What supports it?** List specific evidence (conversations, data, quotes)
2. **What contradicts it?** List counter-evidence, even weak signals
3. **What would disprove it?** Define the falsification criteria
4. **Have we looked for disproving evidence?** Or only confirming evidence?
5. **Classification:** Is this a FACT (directly observed), INFERENCE (derived from evidence), or ASSUMPTION (taken as given)?
6. **Alternative explanations:** Could the same evidence support a different conclusion?

## Bias Checks

- **Confirmation bias:** Are we only collecting evidence that confirms what we already believe?
- **Recency bias:** Are we overweighting the last conversation and underweighting the full evidence body?
- **Authority bias:** Are we treating one person's opinion as proof because of their title?
- **Survivorship bias:** Are we only looking at prospects who responded, not the ones who didn't?
- **Circular reasoning:** Are we using our own positioning as evidence that the positioning is correct?

## Output

Write to `q-system/output/challenge-{date}.md` with:
- Revised confidence levels for each assumption (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW / UNVALIDATED)
- Top 3 weakest assumptions that need evidence or should be dropped
- Recommended next actions to strengthen or disprove weak assumptions

Present to the founder. Do NOT update canonical files from this - that requires /q-calibrate.
